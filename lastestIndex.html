About Us

About our community

ACTS Global Networking (AGN) is a non-profit organization mainly focusing on education and social concern activities. One of the community activities that it has been involved in over the past fifteen years is its “street ministry” where the organization reaches out and helps the hurting and neglected children and youth in Jinjang. Weekly outdoor activities with an average attendance of 100 are conducted to instill moral values in children. Volunteers even make weekly home visits to better respond to each family’s respective needs. AGN has many success stories of these children having progressed to secondary education and a few are even completing their university education through the sponsorship of kind benefactors like HELP Education Group. AGN has been extending support, counseling and meeting the material needs of this needful community for over 15 years.

Our Founder

Founder of AGN



Pastor Raymond Mooi, AGN Founder and Director


A training and equipping school, The School of Acts is founded to teach believers on how to do God’s works in their lives. Servant leadership is emphasized, more than a theological emphasis. Hence, rather than competing with a theological school, The School of Acts complements it by breaking theology open and translating it into immediate and practical application. The hallmark of The School of Acts is witnessing and also experiencing wonders and miracles, rooted in Rev Raymond’s gift of healing. Every year, at the completion of each course, he would bring his students to conduct a mass miracle rally in cities with strongholds. Thousand of people were brought to Christ, where the blind saw, crippled walked and tumours disappeared! “At the The School of Acts, you are prepared in your personal life by getting more breakthroughs and also allow God to change your life in areas you long for. From then on, God can use you in your church where you can be more efficient, effective and stronger,” said Pastor Raymond.

Our Past Events

Past Events of the JinJang Utara Community

  • January 3, 2015

    An Outdoor Christmas Party

    On 3rd of January 2015, Acts Global Networking (AGN) and Church of Acts threw an outdoor Christmas party for 1500 children at Padang Bola Sepak, Kawasan A, Jinjang Utara, Kuala Lumpur. The Christmas party was packed with exciting games, performances, Christmas skits, clowning, magic tricks, festive gift for every child and a Christmas draw. A school bag was donated to each child together with food and gift items totaling to over RM50 per child. YB Chua Tian Chang, YB Hannah Yeoh and Dato’ Chua Jui Meng were the invited guests of the party. YB Hannah Yeoh, honorary guest for the Christmas party, agreed with Rev Raymond, saying that more programs should be developed with the non-governmental organizations (NGO) targeting children in low-cost area to curb social ills before they grow.

  • December 7, 2015

    English Literacy Programme

    During year 2015 end of school holiday, Acts Global Networking (AGN) launched a three-day English literacy programme from 7th to 9th of December for Jinjang Utara Rumah Panjang in collaboration with HELP University (HELP). A total of 140 children of Jinjang Utara aged 6 to 17 learned how to read and write English from the Jolly Phonics English curriculum, some of them for the first time. “Education is one of the few things that does not get wasted, lost or devalued. Not only a child can gain knowledge through education, learning also trains his or her mental discipline. “A better educated child has a better chance in their future career, especially in a time where illiteracy is almost impossible to find,” said Pastor Raymond Mooi, Director of AGN.

  • December 2, 2017

    'Bring Hope' Christmas and Back-to-school Party

    Jinjang, KL – 2 December – The children (and parents) of Jinjang Utara with great delight descended upon Padang Taman Aman Putra where a massive Christmas and Back-to-school party was organised especially for them. Anticipation filled the sunny blue sky, as the children knew their hosts would not disappoint, but supply above and beyond their expectations. As the children are dismissed, they are herded into orderly lines to receive their Christmas presents of school bags, stationery, mugs, and other gifts, all offered joyfully by generous joyful donors. Rev. Raymond Mooi said the reason for today is because they still have so much more to give (apart from the physical blessings), that in Malaysia the context of an occasion is needed, and what better occasion than Christmas. He adds that their work actually goes on throughout the year, and he appreciates the practical power of education going a long way in helping people’s futures. He wonders how the years have passed since their first contact at Jinjang.
